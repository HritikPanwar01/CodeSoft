Albert Einstein

"Imagination is more important than knowledge."

Albert Einstein (1879-1955) was a theoretical physicist, best known for his theory of relativity and his equation, E=mc². He made groundbreaking contributions to quantum mechanics, statistical mechanics, and cosmology, reshaping modern physics. Born in Germany, Einstein had a deep sense of curiosity and a passion for knowledge, which led him to question the conventional views of his time.

Einstein’s work influenced not only science but also philosophy and culture. In 1921, he received the Nobel Prize in Physics for his explanation of the photoelectric effect, further establishing his reputation. Later in life, he advocated for civil rights and was a strong proponent of pacifism.

Timeline of Achievements

  • 1879: Born in Ulm, Germany
  • 1905: Published the theory of special relativity
  • 1915: Published the theory of general relativity
  • 1921: Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics
  • 1933: Moved to the United States, joining the Institute for Advanced Study
  • 1955: Passed away in Princeton, New Jersey
